---
title: The Forest with a Thousand Souls
layout: post
date: 2026-07-11 12:00:00 -0500
tags: microfiction
---

There’s a Forest, where the children run, the adults walk, and the lonely sit.  Some may call it “Ah, it’s just barely a stretch o’ the woods’, while the others say it challenges the redwoods.  Those others grow up and realize but nonetheless.  The mother leads her child along the man-made path.  The child is led by friends on the deer-made path.  Not long after, they are the only one to visit.  Besides the red pipe, they place a doll of mud, hoping the grown-ups will stay out.  It’s a place for only them, the only person that exists in the world.  They ignore the memoirs from those that came before.  The tires, the fence, and the annual wooden decorations for the spooky seasons.  They disregard such items that have assimilated into the Forest.  They crouch beside the decades-old tires, full of grass and water.  Silent conversation begins and sadness is exchanged.  Across the unnatural devices, the question is asked, “Is this what you mean with ‘chill music?’”  The bird calls and, at least, this thousandth soul has the courtesy to keep their music confined so the song of silence cannot be fully interrupted, while they appreciate both.  The Forest awaits the next soul, wondering when the thousandth one will return, if at all.
