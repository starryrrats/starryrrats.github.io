---
title: The Fighter's Hands
layout: post
tags: poetry
---

There lay ten fingers, still and bloody, <br>
But each flaw told a story,<br>
And scars of the past remained,<br>
Living to tell the tale <br>
Of  honor, mercy, and tragedy. <br>

It began with a slight tremble, <br>
No lighter than that of a bug.<br>
As they aged, <br>
They began grasping concepts,<br>
Plucking them out of the air. 

Born with no voice, <br>
Wiggling and curling of the fingers<br>
Must occur for any words<br>
To be spoken,<br>
To be understood. 

With immaturity came foolishness,<br>
For each finger itched for adrenaline.<br>
Their disdain showed through <br>
The flexing and formation of fists,<br>
Ready to engage.

Fingernails were chipped,<br>
As they dug and gouged,<br>
Leaving behind raw wounds.<br>
The scent of iron was strong,<br>
Adding to the air of contempt.

The more these iron fists brawled,<br>
The more the arrogance grew. <br>
Before, they merely defended<br>
But now having dined on the blood,<br>
These fingers could not be sated.

For decades, they reigned<br>
Signing their delicate souls away <br>
To return with rough callouses<br>
All in the name of <br>
Love and war.
