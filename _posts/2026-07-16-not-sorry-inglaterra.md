I saw most of the semifinal match which was Argentina vs England and holy shit there was so much falling.  It's only my fifth or so football match I've seen, but I didn't recall seeing nearly as many guys on the ground like I did with this match. There were 7 fouls for ENG and 12 for Argentina by half time.  I asked my boyfriend what the average amount of fouls were and he said that there’s less but also there were a million fouls that weren’t called out. 

Aaaaand England scored.  But hey, for the rest of the game, apparently their only tactic was to park the bus.  Parking the bus, in case you didn’t know like me, is when you play super defensively, typically after making a leading goal.  It was 1-0 until minute 85.  Then there was a great strike by Fernández, assisted by Messi. Then the second goal was amazing to watch, too. Messi assisted the second goal, by kicking the ball to Martinez who used his head to knock it into the net.

I wasn’t able to watch these goals from a livestream, but even the replays were exciting as hell to watch.  I don’t watch much football so I’m not sure how impressive or not impressive these goals were to the typical viewer, but I was and am amazed at how clean those comeback goals looked.  What a goddamn comeback.  I didn’t think they could get a goal in due to how defensively england was playing, but apparently to play super defensively is not a great tactic and I gotta have more trust in the greatest team with the greatest ever football player. I read some posts and comments about the game and everyone was calling for the resignation of England’s coach for such an embarrassing defeat.  Oof.

So, let’s fucking gooooooooo, Argentina is in the final match now, and they’re against Spain.  There’s a picture circling the Internet again, of a young Messi holding the then-baby Yamal, and now Yamal is playing for Spain and Messi for Argentina.  Neat coincidence.  Good luck Argentina!  Also, Argentina celebrations sound mildly dangerous.  My boyfriend told me he was sore from getting hit during the post-game revelries.  I fear for my hearing and myself if I'm in Argentina when there's another world cup...
