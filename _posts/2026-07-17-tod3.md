---
title: A Stray Bullet
layout: post
tags:
  - microfiction
---
I pass the house and field again. The rusty, red metal targets still blow in the breeze. The field is void except for the crinkling, yellow grass and those damned target plates. They're not riddled with enough holes, but they're still reminiscent of Swiss cheese. Like blood, I'm sure the targets are sharp with iron. They're angry with vengeance and when I'm on my way to pass that house for the last time, he's going to miss those plates and a bullet breaks my car's glass. The window to my left is unbroken, while the right window is shattered. The crack of the gun and the window mixes into a glacial, beautiful harmony and I'm grateful to feel something warm to protect me from the sudden chill.
