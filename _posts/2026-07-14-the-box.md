---
title: The Box
tags:
  - poetry

---


There is a box, <br>
On the step, all alone.<br>
It suddenly appeared one day,<br>
The day after the death.

It’s  wooden I think.<br>
There are holes.<br>
Something makes me reluctant,<br>
Reluctant to even look at it.

I avoid it, until<br>
Curiosity gets the better of my fear.<br>
I stand and gaze,<br>
Inspecting it closely.

Fragile, it says.<br>
This side up,<br>
The arrow points.<br>
Bits of straw cling to it.

Something is in it,<br>
But I’m too scared.<br>
I know there’s something,<br>
Something cruel, something ugly.  
  
The box sits patiently,<br>
As if to taunt me,<br>
As if to tell me:<br>
I’ll wait until you’re ready.

Months pass,<br>
Still, it stays on the step.<br>
I’ve grown used to it,<br>
Used to the presence.

Almost comforting, for it is<br>
The only thing permanent,<br>
During the chaos and grief.<br>
I sit by it, not so afraid now.

One day, I am hit,<br>
Hit with the urge to open it.<br>
But something still holds me back,<br>
Hesitant of what I may find.

But I do it anyway.<br>
I despise and love what I found:<br>
A person, who is me,<br>
Is curled in the box.
