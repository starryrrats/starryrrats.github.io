title: The Box
tags:
  - poetry

---


There is a box,

On the step, all alone.

It suddenly appeared one day,

The day after the death.

  

It’s  wooden I think,

There are holes.

Something makes me reluctant,

Reluctant to even look at it.

  

I avoid it, until

Curiosity gets the better of my fear.

I stand and gaze,

Inspecting it closely.

  

Fragile, it says.

This side up,

The arrow points.

Bits of straw cling to it.

  

Something is in it,

But I’m too scared.

I know there’s something,

Something cruel, something ugly.  
  

The box sits patiently,

As if to taunt me,

As if to tell me:

I’ll wait until you’re ready.

  

Months pass,

Still, it stays on the step.

I’ve grown used to it,

Used to the presence.

  

Almost comforting, for it is

The only thing permanent,

During the chaos and grief.

I sit by it, not so afraid now.

  

One day, I am hit,

Hit with the urge to open it.

But something still holds me back,

Hesitant of what I may find.

  

But I do it anyway.

I despise and love what I found:

A person, who is me,

Is curled in the box.
