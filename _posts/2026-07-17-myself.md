If we were in another life,
We'd be twins, twice the strife.
I'd act as I do now;
I'd boss you around.

I'd have to be your parent.
Remind you of rent,
Clean up your mess,
Oh you'd still give me stress.

But I'd love you all the same,
I'd look at you with disdain,
Yet soften when you cry.
I'd hold you and on the ground, we'd lie.

As I do now, in this other life,
I'd do my best to distract you from the fights.
We'd face everything together.
Separation? Why, simply never.

I hate you and despise you,
But we'd still be bound true,
And for every meltdown of yours
I'd make you tea and set it on the floor.
